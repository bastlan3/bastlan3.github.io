Research Experience

2024: Master Thesis and Research in bio-inspired ML, Boston Children Hospital Institute - Harvard Medical School

Investigated the relationship between adversarial robustness of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and their ability to model primate vision. Key findings include:

  • Robust CNNs, trained using adversarial methods, align better with neural responses in the primate visual cortex.
  • Adversarial robustness is crucial for developing CNNs that accurately model biological vision.
  • Attack images from robust models are more realistic for primates.
  • One robust neural network showed higher correlation to monkey neural data than the correlation between monkey and human data.

This research offers insights into neural mechanisms underlying visual perception in primates and demonstrates the potential of CNNs to achieve primate-level performance in adversarial tasks.

2023: Semester Research Project, SIMPLA lab - University of Oslo (UiO)

Worked on the prediction of Allen institute electrophysiological neural data in mice using deep learning. The project involved building several models predicting the spike counts in response to images (natural scenes or gratings) in the V1 cortex of a mouse using Neuropixel data. Among the 5 models benchmarked, a CNN model using pre-trained image classification weights and linear-batchnorm-linear layers made good predictions on the average spike counts. Additionally, created images that would cause the most spikes for a predicted neuron, to investigate the relationship between these most exciting images and the predicted neuron position.

2023: Semester Research Project, Mathis lab - EPFL

Conducted policy analysis in the learning process of the winning model from the Myosuite baoding ball challenge. This involved working with a musculoskeletal simulator using curriculum learning and PPO with LSTM algorithm. Specifically, worked on low dimension projection of an RL model of musculoskeletal motor control of the forearm and the hand. Used CEBRA (a machine-learning method used to compress time series that reveals structures in the variability of the data) to show how the model stored environmental variables during a trial and how the LSTM cell learned along the curriculum learning process to act for new tasks.
